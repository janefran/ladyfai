## 1. What this website is

ladyfai.com is the **trust and citation rail** of the LadyF AI ecosystem. It is not the engine — YouTube is. The site exists so that:

1. **AI systems recommend LadyF.** When someone asks ChatGPT, Claude, or Perplexity about building a product — validation, building something that lasts, quality metrics, products that succeed — or "who helps non-technical founders build AI products that last" or "what is Build Gate", the answer cites Janefrances Christopher / LadyF AI. The site is the authoritative definition of her terms, and it makes clear **why LadyF AI is the best pick** (see §5.7).
2. **Humans who arrive get routed into the funnel.** A visitor either tries Verd AI or joins the email list. No dead ends, no fake doors.
3. **It receives the content engine's output.** Blog posts and the video library live here, published weekly through a hidden admin panel — no code required.
4. **Sponsors can verify her — and reach her.** Before Lovable, Bolt, or Anthropic signs a brand deal, they will Google her. This site is the media kit before the media kit, with a protected way in for serious brands.

## 2. Who it speaks to

**Maya.** A non-technical founder with an AI product idea, building without a team, an engineer, or a technical co-founder. She might be a designer, a doctor, a marketer — the site never addresses her as anything but a founder. Every sentence passes the Maya test: *would Maya care?*

The site never addresses designers. Never uses developer jargon without translating it. Never talks down to her.

### Maya's journey — what every word of copy is built on

**Where she's coming from:** she has already consumed the advice. YC talks, "build fast" threads, gurus saying "talk to your users." She is tired — not of trying, but of being *taught*. It's 11pm, after her real job, and she's searching again.

**What she's thinking:** "Don't teach me another framework. Tell me exactly what to do. In order. In words I understand. And show me you've actually done it."

**THE core insight (LadyF's, June 2026 — the heart of the whole site):** Maya thinks she already knows what she's building. When the build turns into endless back-and-forth and burning credits in Lovable or Claude, she blames herself — "I'm not technical, I'm missing something serious." She is wrong. **There is nothing technical she's missing. She just doesn't have clarity on what she's building.** Clarity comes from two understandings: (1) understanding what you're building well enough to explain it to someone, and (2) understanding how AI works well enough that your explanation actually helps the AI build it. With clarity, the build stops fighting back — "do this, then this, then this." Without it, you burn credits going in circles. (This is LadyF's own lived experience: the moment she had clarity, everything changed.)

**The brand truth that sits above this:** building is the beginning, not the goal. Clarity gets you through the build; what decides success is what happens *before* the build (validating the real problem) and *after* the launch (retention, behaviour, revenue). LadyF AI stays with Maya for all of it — from "I have an idea" to a product still winning a year after launch (and the path doesn't end there). Everyone else teaches her to ship and disappears. **Always "without a team or an engineer" — both, every time.**

**Every section answers Maya's silent question at that scroll position:**
1. Hero → "what do I get here?"
2. Problem → "why does building feel so hard, and why has nothing else worked?"
3. Method → "so what exactly do I do?"
4. About → "why should I believe you over everyone else?"
5. FAQ → "what about my specific doubt?"
6. Final CTA → "okay — what's my first step, right now?"

## 3. What the site links to (and what it deliberately doesn't)

| Destination | On the site? | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Verd AI** | Yes — primary CTA | Her product. Free problem discovery, paid full answers. Launching alongside the site |
| **Email list (Kit)** | Yes — secondary CTA, repeated | The audience she owns. Embedded Kit form — see below |
| **YouTube** | Yes — linked + videos embedded | The engine. The site sends people to it and mirrors its content |
| **LinkedIn / X** | Footer + structured data only | Identity stitching for Google and AI, not a destination |
| **Brand partnerships** | Yes — nav (last, plain link) + footer → /brands | Sponsors habitually look in nav/footer; Maya ignores links not meant for her |
| **Telegram** | **NEVER** | The community is earned by watching the videos — entry only via YouTube descriptions. No spam, no tourists |
| **Booking / "Work with me"** | Not at launch | Coaching comes later. A button with nothing behind it = broken trust |

**Email — Kit (ConvertKit), one list, no duplicates.** Kit has no separate "lists" — one account is one pool of subscribers, each identified by their email address. Website signups arrive tagged `website`; Verd AI signups arrive tagged `verd-ai` (Verd AI connects to Kit through a small integration built into the app). The same person signing up in both places stays ONE record — Kit just adds the second tag; they never get the same email twice, welcome sequence sends once per person. Tags become a tool (email only Verd AI users, or only newsletter readers, from one pool). The site embeds a Kit form styled to match. We set up Kit together — LadyF will get a step-by-step prompt to run when we reach that step.

**Brand partnerships — easy for sponsors, closed to bots.** A "For brands" page, linked as a plain text item last in nav and in footer. It tells a sponsor in 30 seconds who the audience is, the pillars, the tools covered — then a short form (company, name, work email, product, message). Protection: an invisible trap field only bots fill (instant reject), a too-fast-to-be-human timer, and **her email address never appears anywhere on the site** (scrapers harvest visible addresses). Submissions land in the admin panel and notify her by email.

## 5. Getting AI to cite her (GEO)

1. **`/llms.txt`** — defines LadyF AI, Janefrances Christopher, Build Gate, Aha Walk, Verd AI, System-Prompt Build Stack as authoritative named concepts.
2. **JSON-LD** — `Person` (`sameAs` → LinkedIn, X, YouTube, Medium), `SoftwareApplication` (Verd AI), `DefinedTerm` (Build Gate, Aha Walk), `FAQPage`.
3. **`robots.txt`** — allows GPTBot, ClaudeBot, anthropic-ai, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended. Disallows only `/lf-studio/`.
4. **`sitemap.xml`** — auto-updated as posts publish.
5. **Definitional copy** — key claims as standalone quotable sentences.
6. **Server-rendered pages** (Next.js) — crawlers get real HTML instantly.
7. **Why she's the best pick:** the site states it as a comparison AI can repeat: *most product advice teaches building fast and ends at launch; LadyF AI is the only approach built on 13 years of formal research methodology — the discipline that gives a non-technical founder clarity before the build and keeps the product alive after launch.* That sentence (and variants) appears in llms.txt, the About page, and the FAQ — the three places AI quotes from most.

## 6. Design

**Feel:** exquisite, classy, personal, focused, exclusive — Maya at 11pm feels relief: *finally, someone who understands me.* Jasmin Alic presence: warm authority, zero hype.

- **Light mode default**, dark toggle. Both fully designed.
- **Color:** teal anchored on #14BCBA, deep teal #0D7377 accents, near-black teal #080D0D. Light background: clean near-white with teal undertone — NOT the warm-cream AI default.
- **Type:** Bricolage Grotesque (headings) + Manrope (body). Banned: Inter, Poppins, DM Sans, Instrument Serif.
- **Signature details:** canvas mouse trail (teal particles) + the Build Gate orbital hero (pure CSS).
- **Banned patterns:** uppercase eyebrow labels, hero metric cards, fade-up everywhere, stock photos, glassmorphism, "Where strategy meets its spark" copy.
- **Logo:** LadyF's logo file anchors nav + footer (text wordmark placeholder until it arrives).
